Orange Star Commanders

Andy

Andy is an Orange Star CO whose lack of any strengths or weaknesses allows for more versatile play. As such, Andy is a good CO choice for beginners.

Power Bar
Strength None
Weakness None
Day-to-Day Effects
None. All of Andy's units have regular offense and defense.
Power: Hyper Repair
Restores 2 HP to all of his Units and increases their defense by 10% (And offense by 10% in Dual Strike).
Super Power: Hyper Upgrade
Restores 5 HP to all of his Units, increases firepower by 30%, defense by 10%, and movement by 1 space.

Max

Max is a direct-combat specialist, possesing significant firepower bonuses to ground, air, and naval direct-attack units. The added firepower is usually enough to overwhelm most other CO's directs and put armies on the defensive, especially on maps spacious enough for direct units to roam free.

This advantage is offset by range and firepower penalties for his indirects, usually putting Max into situations where he must gain a decisive advantage before the opposing army fully develops and pushes him back with superior indirects.

Power Bar
Strength Direct Combat
Weakness Indirect Combat
Day-to-Day Effects
All of Max's Direct Combat units have 20% increased offense, and all of his Indirect Combat units suffer -1 Range.
Power: Max Force
Firepower of all non-infantry Direct Combat is increased by 40%. All units gain a 10% defence bonus.
Super Power: Max Blast
Firepower of all non-infantry Direct Combat is increased by 60%. All units gain a 10% defence bonus.

Sami

Sami is a CO who specializes in Foot soldiers and capturing properties. Her increased capture speed will allow her to take contested properties more easily, and her high offensive boost for foot soldiers will ensure any enemy Infantry will take significant damage while she is capturing.

Power Bar
Strength Infantry, Transports
Weakness Direct Combat
Day-to-Day Effects
All of Sami's foot soldiers have 20% increased offense and capture properties 50% faster (rounded down). Direct combat vehicles have 10% decreased offense.
Power: Double Time
Increases movement range by one for all Infantry and Mechs by 1 space, and their firepower is raised by 60%. They also receive a 10% defence bonus. All other Units get a 10% boost in firepower and defence.
Super Power: Victory March
Increases movement range of Infantry and Mech units by 2 spaces. All foot soldiers gain a 90% increase in firepower and may capture in one turn, regardless of HP.

Jake

Jake is a CO that specializes in fighting on Plains. Jake is a good CO choice for beginners, as his powers offer him a degree of flexibility in play, while having no real weaknesses.

He is solid on many common maps, with no inherent weaknesses and better than average offensive power on plains. One of the most interesting aspects of Jake is not actually his passive abilities, though. His CO Power charges fairly early, and with it you get an okay attack boost added to plains on top of his passive, but usually more importantly, a boost to the range of your indirect units.

Power Bar
Strength Plains Combat
Weakness None
Day-to-Day Effects
All of Jake's units have 10% increased offense if they are fighting on a Plains tile. This bonus extends to Air units as well.
Power: Beat Down
All of Jake's Land Indirect units gain +1 Range. All of Jake's units gain 20% increased offense when fighting on Plains, 10% otherwise. Jake's units gain 10% increased defense as well.
Super Power: Block Rock
All of Jake's Land Indirect units gain +1 range, and all of Jake's Land vehicles gain +2 movement. All of Jake's units gain 40% increased offense when fighting on Plains, 10% otherwise. Jake's units gain 10% increased defense as well.

Rachel

Rachel is a CO with a diverse set of bonuses, allowing higher potential for both aggressive and defensive play. Her Units are hardworking which allows her units to increase base repairs.

Power Bar
Strength Increased Repair Speed
Weakness None
Day-to-Day Effects
All of Rachel's properties heal units for up to 3 HP damage, instead of up to 2 HP damage. Rachel is still liable for the repair costs.
Power: Lucky Lass
Rachel gains increased Luck (Luck range is set to +0% to +39%). All of Rachel's units gain 10% increased offense and defense.
Super Power: Covering Fire
All of Rachel's units gain 10% increased offense and defense. Three missiles with the same range/damage as silos are fired. These missiles are aimed automatically, targeted, respectively as follow:
  • The first missile targets the largest group of Infantry it can find, with priority given to capturing Infantry.
  • The second missile does as much damage in funds as it can. The value of Rachel's own troops is subtracted from this calculation.
  • The third and final missile does as much HP damage as it possibly can, but will do the most damage in funds if there is a tie. The HP Rachel's own troops would lose is subtracted from this calculation.

Nell

Nell is a CO whose lucky nature allows her to occasionally do significantly more damage on any given attack than expected.

Power Bar
Strength High Luck
Weakness None
Day-to-Day Effects
All of Nell's units have increased luck (0% to +19%). Otherwise, all of Nell's units are average, with no strengths or weaknesses.
Power: Lucky Star
Nell's units have an increased Luck Cap (0% to 59%). Nell's units gain 10% increased offense and defense.
Super Power: Covering Fire
Nell's units have a further increased Luck Cap (0% to 99%). Nell's units gain 10% increased offense and defense.

Bear in mind, luck is still luck. That damage counter may actually be right, and that could spell the end of your brave little recon.
